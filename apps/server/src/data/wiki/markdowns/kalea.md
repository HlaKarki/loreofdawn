## Story

> "Descendent of the Great Serpent that swims gracefully through the Vonetis Sea with her serpent tail."

Kalea is the descendant of the legendary Great Serpent. She once had trouble accepting her human-like body, but after discovering who she was, she learned to fight with her fists and powerful serpent tail while healing allies with the power of the Great Serpent surging inside her. Always full of energy, she is the proud protector of the Vonetis Sea. However, when faced with gratitude from others, she tends to put on a facade of arrogance to hide her shyness—after all, she has to live up to her reputation as the descendant of the Great Serpent!

Related heroes: Kadita, Badang

## Lore

Out in the Vonetis Sea, an island called Solari Isle was facing the storm of the century. As the waves rose higher and higher, muffled thunder rumbled through the gray clouds.

The villagers on the island were in a panic—the typhoon had come without warning and one fishing boat had not yet returned from the sea. They prayed to an ancient idol, hoping that the girl with the blood of the Great Serpent would hear them and bring their kin back safely.

That girl's name was Kalea.

The children, hugging their toys made in the image of the Great Serpent, began to sing a song.

> "The wind howls; the waves roar. O Great Serpent, mighty as a mountain, hold back the raging tides..."

Their singing gradually faded in the increasingly fierce storm. Suddenly, a serpent tail swept through the waves and headed towards the depths.

Legend has it that a thousand years ago, a gigantic serpent emerged from the ocean to protect this island. It swallowed the typhoons and held off waves as high as the mountains with its body until it had exhausted all of its power. Before the Great Serpent perished, it left behind an egg. A thousand years later, a girl miraculously hatched from the egg. She had a human body, a gift from the land, and a sea serpent's tail, a blessing from the ocean. The goddess Kadita named her Kalea and took her to the sea where she secretly watched her grow up.

> "Oh Mister Sea Turtle, why do you swim so slowly?"

Kalea joked, as she wrapped her tail around the sea turtle and flung it across the sea with surprising strength. After seeing the splash way in the distance, she nodded with satisfaction.

> "You're welcome!"

> "Silly sharks, why are you so weak?"

Kalea spun her tail at great speed, stirring up a strong undercurrent. The sharks were tossed up and down, but Kalea effortlessly swam off.

Kalea had awakened the power of the Great Serpent at an early age, but she was never able to control it. Her power would surge outwards without warning, turning the seas upside down, much to the dismay of the creatures living there. Even Kalea began to worry. Every time she tried to channel the full power of the Great Serpent, it would disperse out of her control.

Kalea firmly believed that it was her weak, human-like body that was holding her back:

> "Look at those feeble humans. They're slower than the turtles, and even scared of the sharks!"

If only she didn't have these bothersome arms and legs, she thought, then she could swim even faster! If she could finally take the form of the Great Serpent, no monster in the sea or on land would be a match for her! Oh, how she longed for that day. Kadita, who had been secretly watching over her, appeared and explained that she could not just shed her skin like a serpent. Unwilling to accept Kadita's words, Kalea challenged all manner of powerful creatures, hoping to prove herself worthy of the Great Serpent's power.

One day, while wandering the beaches of Solari Isle, Kalea was attacked by a swarm of corrupted elementals. Hearing the commotion, the villagers, led by Badang, came to her rescue. Badang erected giant walls of sand with his fists to split the enemy, while the villagers threw spears and fishing nets to subdue them. Ultimately, their combined efforts were successful in defeating the rogue elementals.

> "These humans... maybe they're not so weak after all..."

Kalea began to secretly study how Badang fought with his fists as she quietly learned to peel bananas with her fingers. As the descendant of the Great Serpent, she was always reluctant to lower herself to learn from mortals, yet she couldn't help but be curious about their peculiar lives. Even though she tried to keep her distance from the villagers, she found her power was inexplicably connected to them. Whenever her power surged outward, it would always seek out those in need and heal them.

> "I'm just here to test my strength against Badang!"

Kalea would explain as she appeared more and more often around Solari Isle. Badang would always smile helplessly and act defeated. He saw from their sparring that the girl's heart was no longer set on just proving her strength.

Knocking down a whole coconut tree with one punch, freeing a stranded fishing boat with her tail—such acts were not just for her pride. When the villagers gathered to thank her, Kalea would always put her hands on her hips and proudly say something like,

> "I wasn't trying to help you, I was just bored and exercising my tail!"

But all the while, the villagers could see her face blushing from the attention.

No matter how much she tried, Kalea was no closer to becoming the Great Serpent. Her body barely grew at all, but despite that, the villagers began turning to her for help, just as they had once turned to her ancestor in times of need; times like now.

A tail sliced through the waves at extreme speed. On the surface, the howling wind caused the waves to surge to towering heights. A lone fishing boat, caught in the storm, was lifted high into the air by the waves before being slammed down with great force.

With a splash, Kalea leaped to action. She grabbed the side of the boat and gently deposited the fallen fishermen back on board with her tail.

> "Humans really are fragile! But you're pretty brave to be out here, even when you don't have a tail!"

The Great Serpent's power surged from her body and flowed into the fishermen, causing them to cough up the seawater they swallowed. Miraculously, they regained their strength as if nothing had happened.

> "I'm sorry the boat can't be saved, but you two will get to witness the power of the Great Serpent!"

Without waiting for a reply, Kalea grabbed the hands of the fishermen and dove off the boat as she zoomed towards the shore.

With her tail, she bounced over the waves, riding the wind and breaking through the surf. Kalea saw her reflection flickering in the parting waters; her tailing, swaying with purpose, and her hands, holding tightly onto the fishermen. That was who she was. She was no longer fixated on becoming the Great Serpent. As for the power that she once sought to contain, she finally realized she had already found its purpose.

> "I guess this body isn't so bad after all!"

With the turbulent waves behind her, the shore came into sight. A child hugging a toy of the Great Serpent excitedly pointed towards the sea and shouted:

> "Look! It's Kalea!"

With her serpent tail, this proud descendant of the Great Serpent swims gracefully through the Vonetis Sea, aspiring to one day carry the full power of her heritage.

## Abilities

### Passive — Surge of Life
- Tags / Related: Water Zone, Winter Crown, Phoveus, Demonic Force  
- Scales / stats: (various scaling entries present) Base HP, PA/PD scalings listed in source
- Description: After casting a skill, Kalea creates a Water Zone on the ground. When Kalea casts a skill within this zone, she will absorb it to enhance her next three Basic Attacks with different effects:
  - First Basic Attack: Kalea dashes to the target, dealing damage.
  - Second Basic Attack: Kalea unleashes a flurry of attacks, dealing damage and recovering HP for herself and the nearby lowest HP ally.
  - Third Basic Attack: Kalea uses her tail to strike the area around her target, dealing damage and creating a new Water Zone.
- Notes / Additional mechanics:
  - Enhanced basic attacks last as a 12-second buff while there are unused enhanced attacks. Each enhanced attack can be manually halted. The first enhanced attack increases attack range to 3.2 and slightly knocks back the target. The second enhanced attack cannot be manually canceled and takes ~1.2s to execute; it launches 4 projectiles that heal a nearby ally (within ~7.5 units). If the enemy dies or becomes untargetable, the heal will not occur but the animation persists and the third enhanced attack is reserved.
  - Water Zone properties: each zone lasts 6 seconds, combines with another if created while inside an existing zone, impossible to have two zones at once, gives vision within 9 units, and reveals bushes when created inside them.

### Skill 1 — Wavebreaker
- Tags: AOE, Water Zone
- Damage scaling: Base Damage by level (180 / 200 / 220 / 240 / 260 / 280)
- Cooldown: 4.0s
- Description: Kalea slams her fist into the ground, creating a wave that deals damage to nearby enemies and slows them by 40% for 1.5s.
- Notes: The wave has a radius of 3 (slightly shorter than her first enhanced basic attack).

### Skill 2 — Tidal Strike
- Tags: Mobility, CC, Water Zone
- Damage scaling: Two instances of Base Damage by level (150 / 175 / 200 / 225 / 250 / 275)
- Cooldown: 13.0 → 11.0s across levels (13.0 / 12.6 / 12.2 / 11.8 / 11.4 / 11.0)
- Description: Kalea charges forward and body-slams the first enemy hero she encounters, knocking them airborne for 0.6s while dealing damage. If she collides with an obstacle or touches the edge of a Water Zone during her charge, Kalea will leap into the air. Cast the skill again to slam down at a target location, dealing damage and knocking enemies airborne for 0.6s.
- Notes:
  - Fully affects concealed and invisible enemies.
  - After jumping to a wall or obstacle, she has ~2 seconds to select the landing area. If CC'd while perched, she may be forced down.
  - The cooldown begins when the ability is cast.
  - The landing animation can be blocked or repositioned by certain enemy skills. This ability triggers her passive twice.

### Ultimate — Tsunami Slam
- Tags: CC, related: Kalea, Phoveus, Demonic Force, Diversion, Minsitthar, King's Calling
- Damage scaling: Base Damage 300 / 400 / 500 (levels)
- Cooldown: 55.0 / 50.0 / 45.0s
- Description: Kalea grabs an enemy hero with her tail and slams them down in the joystick's direction, dealing damage to the target and nearby enemies.
- Notes / Mechanics:
  - Kalea is CC Immune for the entire duration.
  - Max distance is 7.5 units.
  - Two phases: she first grabs (stun), then applies airborne while moving the target with her. On landing, she creates a Water Zone, loses CC immunity, and dashes toward the joystick direction ~1.5 units, triggering her passive twice.
  - The landing area is visible only to Kalea. She cannot use other abilities, battle spells, or equipment during the ultimate. Both Kalea and the target can be displaced if the landing area is over an obstacle.

## Trivia
- The name Kalea is of Hawaiian origin, meaning "brightness" or "clear." Her design and inspiration are taken from Southeast Asian cultures.
- Her design features an aquamarine color scheme, reflecting coastal waters and emphasizing her role as protector of the Vonetis Sea.
- Her stance represents an S shape symbolizing the Great Serpent.
- She loves coconuts; it's her favorite.
- She and another (unnamed here) are currently the only heroes with a Support/Fighter hybrid role. (See: Support / Fighter)
