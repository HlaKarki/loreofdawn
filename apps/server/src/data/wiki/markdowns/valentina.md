## Story

> "Former head of House Paxley who'd stop at nothing to revive her lover."

In a battle against the Abyss invasion, Valentina and her lover, Irlan, were betrayed by some members of House Paxley, which eventually caused Irlan to lose his soul. To revive her lover, Valentina made a deal with an Evil God (Kalon) and gained immortality in the power of shadow. Since then, she'd been hiding among the shadows, silently waiting for the appearance of the Heart of Anima — a broken piece from the Twilight Orb that was said to be the key to retrieving her lover's soul.

## Lore

House Paxley ruled over the regions near the Lantis Mountains in the south of the Moniyan Empire. Generations of the house had guarded these areas for the Empire, defending against the invasion of the Abyss with magic and swords.

As the house patriarch's eldest daughter, Valentina had been given high hopes since she was born. She underwent rigorous training and grew up into a proud perfectionist, the heir most qualified to lead this noble house. As she grew older, she went to the frontier and helped the house repel wave after wave of invading demons. The news of victories in the Lantis Mountains became the only thing that could put the civilians' minds at ease. As a result, House Paxley rose quickly among the nobles, while Valentina became the youngest and only female member of the house council.

In search of allies, Valentina went alone to Moonlit Forest despite the Empire's prolonged hostility toward the Elves. The Elf King, Estes, refused Valentina's request, saying the Elves had no interest in humans' warfare. An Elf hero named Irlan, however, was moved by Valentina's sincere words. Unlike Estes, he believed that with the fall of the human empire, the demise of Moonlit Forest would also be imminent. A few heated words were exchanged, after which Irlan followed Valentina out of Moonlit Forest with his friends. Elves and humans had since fought alongside each other on the battlefields of the Lantis Mountains.

After the days and nights spent with Valentina, putting their lives at risk, Irlan realized that he'd fallen desperately in love with her. But Valentina was at a loss when Irlan revealed his feelings for her: she'd always been taught that a noble lady shouldn't indulge herself in ridiculous romance, and she should marry someone of equal status for the interest of the house. Nonetheless, Irlan's passionate courtship awakened the feelings buried deep in Valentina's heart. She felt the wonderful joy of being loved for the very first time.

At a House Paxley election meeting, Irlan proposed that he would fight under Valentina's command permanently on the condition that she accept his marriage proposal. Valentina accepted, and they shared vows under the moonlight; Valentina became head of the house while her uncle, who expected the position, remained silent.

With the assistance of the Elves, House Paxley gained power each day, and Valentina was conferred the title of Duchess by the Moniyan Empire. She relied on Irlan's love and, having grown used to facing death, finally tasted the sweetness of life.

Disaster struck when the Abyss gathered strength and attacked House Paxley's fief. Aware the house's force could not match the Abyss army, Valentina resorted to a forbidden spell called the "Soul Casket": a ritual that would create a deadly zone and devour all souls within it, sparing no one, demon or human. Valentina and Irlan led the front line to cover the mages preparing the spell. When the Soul Casket symbol appeared over the battlefield, the withdrawal signal never came — Valentina discovered she had been betrayed by her uncle and the mages.

Valentina shouted for retreat; at the last moment before the casket fell, Irlan pushed Valentina out of the zone with his final strength, then fell as he lost his soul. The Lantis Battle ended, and Valentina's legend grew, but she knew the true price of victory. She executed those who had betrayed her and escorted Irlan's body back to Castle Aberleen. She built a sacred hall in commemoration of Irlan and the martyrs and dedicated her life to finding a way to restore Irlan's soul.

In her desperation to bring him back, Valentina turned to black magic and awakened a trace of the consciousness of the Evil God of Trickery, Kalon. Kalon told Valentina of the Heart of Anima — a broken piece from the Twilight Orb — and tricked her into signing a blood pact. By the pact, Valentina gained immortality and shadow powers, but all souls of House Paxley became tainted by Kalon; when his power fully awoke, he would be revived through a Paxley descendant.

To prevent Kalon's return, Valentina left a prophecy telling her descendants that if the Cursed One ever appeared, they must destroy that person at all costs. Since then, Valentina has hidden in the shadows, waiting for the Heart of Anima and the day she might retrieve her loved one's soul.

## Abilities

### Passive

- EXP received = 12.5 + (2.5 × hero level)
- HP regen = damage dealt × [30% + (2% × hero level)]
- Damage type: Magic Damage
- Lifesteal type: Magic Lifesteal

Primal Force
- Effects: BUFF, HEAL
- Cooldown: 2.0 s
- Description: Valentina gains 8–50 extra EXP each time she deals damage to an enemy hero. If the enemy hero's level isn't higher than Valentina's, 30% of the damage dealt is converted into her HP. The EXP scales with Valentina's level.
- EXP increase (by Valentina level): 8 / 11 / 14 / 17 / 20 / 23 / 26 / 29 / 32 / 35 / 38 / 41 / 44 / 47 / 50
- Notes:
  - Only damage dealt by Valentina's own skills benefits from the 30% HP conversion.
  - Full EXP is gained when the damage is from emblems, battle spells, basic attacks but not on non-minion enemies such as summons.
  - Damage from battle spells and equipment cannot be converted to HP.

- Type: Magic Skill (Damage, CC)
- Cooldown: 7.0 / 6.6 / 6.2 / 5.8 / 5.4 / 5.0 s
- Mana cost: 70 / 75 / 80 / 85 / 90 / 95
- Base damage: 340 / 405 / 470 / 535 / 600 / 665
- Description: Valentina casts Shadow Strike in the target direction, dealing magic damage to enemies hit and slowing them by 40% for 1 second. Enemy heroes hit are marked with a Shadow Sigil for 4 seconds; if hit again by Shadow Strike within that duration, they will be terrified for 1 second. A unit can only be terrified once within 4 seconds.

- Type: Blink, Magic Damage Skill
- Cooldown: 12.0 s
- Mana cost: 45 / 50 / 55 / 60 / 65 / 70
- Base damage (per Shadow Bolt): 125 / 145 / 165 / 185 / 205 / 225
- Description: Valentina dashes in the target direction and fires 3 Shadow Bolts at nearby enemies, each bolt dealing the listed base damage. She can cast this skill again within 6 s (costs Mana). The cooldown of this skill is reduced by 1 second each time a Shadow Bolt hits an enemy.
- Notes:
  - This skill can target hidden enemy units.

### Ultimate — I Am You

- Name: I Am You
- Effects: Morph, Slowed
- Cooldown: Varying (not less than 10 s; CD rate scales)
- Mana cost: Varying
- Duration of stolen form: up to 12 s
- CD rate scaling: 120% / 110% / 100% (results in cooldown being 120%–100% of the target's ultimate cooldown)
- Stolen ultimate skill level: equals Valentina's ultimate level (1 / 2 / 3)
- Steal range: 7
- Description: Valentina slows a target enemy hero by 70% for 0.5 s and gains the ability to cast that hero's Ultimate. Casting it turns Valentina into the enemy hero's form and allows her to inherit their Basic Attack type for the duration (her regular skills remain unchanged unless the enemy Ultimate has a Morph effect). The stolen Ultimate's cooldown is a percentage of the target's cooldown (see CD rate), but never lower than 10 s. If the stolen hero is a Physical Damage hero, Valentina gains related benefits for the duration.
- Notes (selected):
  - The stolen Ultimate's level equals Valentina's ultimate level.
  - Duration ends either when the skill duration expires or when manually canceled.
  - The copied Ultimate may include stacks and buffs from the original hero; many summoned units or persistent effects will end when the copy ends or when Valentina dies.
  - Valentina cannot steal from another Valentina.
  - Some ultimates with multi-phase forms or transformations may only copy the default/first phase.
  - Skins and some visual effects of the copied hero can be copied as well.
  - The stolen ultimate's cooldown begins when the ability is cast (from a minimum of 10 s as applicable).

## Trivia

- She is the first hero that is able to copy an enemy's skill.
- She is the first hero that has an ability related to experience level.
- If both teams pick the same hero, she may participate in duplicated hero forms at the same time, enabling stacked ultimate effects impossible in normal matches.
- Due to a bug prior to Patch 1.6.42, she could copy heroes' icons in battle.
- Her release was postponed due to delays in Patch 1.6.34 (Android/iOS/App Store review delays), affecting related events including Starlight Fest and Black Friday events.
