# Story

> "A graceful, free-soaring Finch and the Oriental Fighter who inherited the Vermillion Bird's power."

Ling once belonged to the Finch Sect, a mysterious and feared order of assassins from the Cadia Riverlands. During a mission, he nearly lost his life but was saved by the Vermilion Bird; it was an encounter that would kindle his yearning for freedom. In a bargain to leave the Finch Sect, he undertook one last mission to infiltrate the Dragon Altar.

It wasn't until he allied with Yu Zhong, the reincarnation of the Black Dragon, to break the seal of the Dragon Altar, that the spirit of the Vermilion Bird once again saved his life. Only then did he see the truth and understand the meaning of freedom. In this world, the Vermilion Bird had faded away, leaving only Ling, a young man with wings to soar freely among the clouds like the Cyan Finch.

# Side Story

## I am the Cyan Finch

> "From now on, you are the Vermilion Bird. When the bird's cry echoes through the Dragon Altar, you will soar."

Ling was not like the other members of the Finch Sect—different in talent and circumstance. And he was treated differently by the elder.

The Finch Sect, an order of assassins from the Cadia Riverlands, takes in orphans, training and honing them to be renowned killers. The disciples of Finch Sect are numerous, each with their own reasons for fighting. Some are blindly loyal to the Finch Sect, some were never given a choice, and others are driven by the pursuit of power... Whatever the reason, once one becomes a Finch of the Sect, their life belongs to it. Not a single Finch has ever left the nest.

Ling became the prodigy of the Finch Sect at a young age. His skills had quickly surpassed his peers and he was known to have never failed a mission. The other members would often whisper:

> "That man is not like us. Perhaps one day, he will leave the Finch Sect and spread his wings."

But... what then? A young Finch hesitantly interjected.

He was met with silence. It was a good question. If they left the Sect, where could they go?

At that moment, Ling sheathed his sword and leaped away. As the wind brushed his face, he suddenly recalled something from the past.

When Ling was on a mission in his younger years, he encountered a bird merchant. The merchant raised beautiful birds for others to enjoy. But these birds were caged from birth and never experienced the freedom that was their natural right. Even if the cage door was opened, they wouldn't know where to go. At that time, a young man nearby suddenly said,

> "Something born with wings belongs in the sky. What a pity."

Later, Ling became friends with that young man, who turned out to be a disciple of the Tianyin Swordmaster Sect named Zilong.

The next time he met Zilong, Ling had changed his identity.

That day, the Finch Sect elder summoned Ling, and behind heavy curtains, uttered only four words: "The time has come." The next day, Ling was sent on an impossible mission—to assassinate the guardian of the Dragon Altar, Baxia. Ling's sword was resolute, but Baxia was too formidable. Ling was no match for the veteran who survived the Battle of the Twin Dragons, yet he couldn't turn back. On the verge of death, Ling suddenly heard birds chirping and saw a flickering of flames; a fiery bird appeared in front of him. Its appearance even caused Baxia to shift into his true form, the Mystic Tortoise.

> "...The Vermilion Bird?!"

It worked. As long as Baxia believed he was the Vermilion Bird, he wouldn't kill Ling.

Ling soon lost consciousness.

He saw the fiery bird again. In the darkness, they looked at each other in silence. From the elder, from Baxia, and from the scriptures in the Finch Sect, he knew it was the Vermilion Bird. Since childhood, whenever Ling was on the brink of death, the bird would appear before him and shield him from disaster. Ling did not know where it came from, for it never spoke. He only knew this familiar dark space and flickering flame.

When he woke up, Baxia took him to see Zilong—the successor of the Cloud Dragon. Together, they returned to the Dragon Altar.

Life at the Dragon Altar with Baxia and Zilong was peaceful, with most days spent practicing martial arts. Baxia was rarely seen, so Ling spent most of his time with Zilong. Zilong practiced his spear daily without slack, but whenever they sparred, Zilong seldom won. After one match, Ling couldn't help but ask Zilong about why he practiced his spear. Zilong did not have an answer. And in that moment, tumultuous waves surged in Ling's heart—did Zilong really not have a reason... or did he never need one, for he simply followed his heart.

This feeling... was it envy? Resentment? Self-pity? Ling's expression remained calm as he effortlessly changed the subject.

The answer no longer mattered, for this game was coming to an end. That night, Ling received orders from the Finch Sect elder to locate a forbidden seal in the back mountains of the Dragon Altar.

After some searching, Ling had found it. The moment Ling touched the seal, he heard a clear and long shriek; a bird of fire suddenly illuminated the darkness. When he woke up from the apparent hallucination, he saw that Baxia had caught him in the act. Without exchanging words, they began a fierce battle until Zilong arrived and put a stop to their clash with his spear.

That was the first time Ling witnessed the might of the Cloud Dragon; the divine power of the Four Immortals.

The Four Immortals have protected the Cadia Riverlands since the time of the Endless War. The four have since fallen silent, and their power has been passed down among the disciples of the Great Dragon. Once a mortal becomes an Oriental Fighter, they naturally assume the responsibility of protecting the Cadia Riverlands. Baxia repeated these principles daily during morning lessons, unaware that Ling had already read and memorized them in the Finch Sect archives.

Ling knew what the elder was after, just as he knew what Baxia was after, but Ling felt they were both mistaken.

If a caged bird couldn't even save itself, how could it save others?

Ling returned to the Finch Sect. The elder did not ask whether Ling succeeded in his mission, nor was he surprised about Ling's return. The assassins of the Finch Sect all eventually return to their nest. But Ling returned with something lingering in his heart. A few years later, Yu Zhong and Luo Yi approached the Finch Sect to form an alliance with the elder and attack the Dragon Altar. Ling volunteered to join.

Zilong had become stronger. He had learned to fully control the Cloud Dragon's power, and Ling was defeated.

For the first time, Ling felt desperation. He unsheathed his sword again and launched attack after attack—

> "Do you wield your sword for your mission?"

Ling had no reply.

> "I wield my spear for me. Who is it you're drawing your blade for?"

Zilong stepped aside.

> "Go, it's waiting for you."

Ling found himself back in that dark place again, facing the fiery bird from afar.

When the seal of the Dragon Altar was weakened, the successors of the Cloud Dragon and the Agile Tiger had already been found, but the Vermilion Bird suffered a grave injury in its struggle to escape. Only a portion of its spirit made it out and was resting in the forest near Shanshui City. After encountering a young Ling on the brink of death, the Vermilion Bird decided to pass its incomplete power to him to save his life, sealing its own fate.

> "I am not the one who is caged."

> "With a caged heart, everywhere is a prison. With an unbound heart, nothing can cage you."

It was not a mission or some kind of exchange. The last wish of the Vermilion Bird's remnant spirit was for Ling to soar freely between heaven and earth, as a bird should. The seemingly heavy burden of the mission could also be entrusted on the weight of a feather. Saving himself from death and protecting the Cadia Riverlands carried the same significance. The power the Vermilion Bird granted him was neither a weapon of death nor a symbol of salvation, but—

Ling heard the sound of something cracking. The bird flapped its wings one last time and the darkness began to crack. A light fell on Ling, igniting the blue flames within him that shot out like a pair of wings.

This was the Vermilion Bird's final dance... and the Cyan Finch's first flight.
