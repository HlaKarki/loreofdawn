# Story

# Abilities

## Nether Touch

Vexana inflicts Nether Curse on enemies hit. The Curse lasts 5 seconds and causes the affected enemy to explode upon death, dealing damage based on level and Vexana's magic power to nearby enemies.

- Base Damage by data level: 108 / 136 / 164 / 192 / 220 / 248 / 276 / 304 / 332 / 360 / 388 / 416 / 444 / 472 / 500

- The damage from Vexana's basic attack applies and refreshes the debuff on nearby enemies.

- Curse duration: 5 seconds.

- Explosion damage scales with Vexana's level and magic power.

## Deathly Grasp

Vexana unleashes a projectile in the target direction, dealing magic damage to enemies in its path. The projectile stops upon hitting an enemy data, terrifying them and knocking them back. It then explodes, dealing magic damage to nearby enemies and terrifying them for 1 second.

- Base Damage by level: 200 / 220 / 240 / 260 / 280 / 300
- Explosion Damage by level: 200 / 220 / 240 / 260 / 280 / 300
- Terrify duration on explosion: 1 second
- Projectile stops on first enemy data hit and inflicts knockback and terrify

Notes:
- Caster is immune to crowd control while using the skill but this does not remove lingering terrify effects on enemies.
- The skill is a magic damage skill and has a high crowd control effect.

## Cursed Blast

Vexana marks a target area with the power of the undead, striking it after a 0.8 second delay and dealing magic damage to enemies hit.

- Base Damage by level: 580 / 634 / 689 / 743 / 797 / 850
- Delay before strike: 0.8 seconds

Notes:
- This is an area of effect magic damage skill that scales with Vexana's magic power.

## Eternal Guard

Vexana summons an Eternal Guard at the target location, dealing initial magic damage to enemies hit and knocking them airborne for 0.8 seconds. The Eternal Guard then joins Vexana in battle for 15 seconds. Each of the Eternal Guard's attacks deals area magic damage.

- Base Summon Damage by level: 500 / 600 / 700
- Guard Slash Damage by level: 225 / 250 / 275
- Summon duration: 15 seconds
- Knockup duration on summon: 0.8 seconds

Notes:
- Vexana cannot have more than one Eternal Guard; summoning a new one will cause the older one to disappear.
- Death prevents the recall of the Eternal Guard.
- Eternal Guard benefits 100% of Vexana's attributes except for its own HP. It is fully affected by Vexana's attribute scaling and equipment attribute effects.
- Eternal Guard's attacks are magic damage effects and are not treated as basic attacks or skill effects for certain interactions.
- Its attack speed cannot be increased or decreased by external effects; its attack speed is approximately between 1.1 and 1.2.
- The Guard prioritizes heroes in range and will attack the heroes that Vexana targets. It can attack jungle creeps if no higher priority targets are available, but it will never secure last hits.

# Trivia

- Her full name is Vexana Gaius.
- The name "Vexana" may come from the Latin word "vexare," which means to torment, distress, or harass. This could symbolize her ability to curse, harm, and control enemies through her necromantic powers, fitting her role as a necromancer who manipulates the spirits of the dead and inflicts torment on her foes.
- Her scepter belonged to Duke Atticus, her fiancé, who was corrupted on their supposed wedding day.
- The souls of Atticus and brave fighters are present in the undead knight.
- In Project NEXT, a major update aimed at reworking and balancing older heroes in Mobile Legends: Bang Bang, Vexana was one of the heroes revamped alongside several others.
- Vexana is the first data to receive a Zenith-grade skin in Mobile Legends: Bang Bang. Her "Twisted Fairytale" skin, introduced between March 25 and April 30, 2024, marked the debut of this high-rarity skin category.
- Zenith skins appear to be fairytale-themed. Vexana's Zenith skin, "Twisted Fairytale," is heavily inspired by Snow White.
- Lord Gaius and Emperor Gaius are different: Lord Gaius is the title and surname of Vexana's father, while Emperor Gaius is the title and name of the first ruler of the Empire of Monian, from whom the surname "Gaius" came.
