## Story

> Innocent princess that grew up in the Swan Castle and genius mage that excels in sword and scepter.

As the daughter of Prince Alvin, Princess Odette appeared to be a well-behaved, obedient lady but actually yearned for adventures. After she became acquainted with Lancelot and rescued him during a duel ruckus, Odette slowly became aware of her feelings toward him. Finally, she decided to leave the Swan Castle and set out on an adventure in search of her loved one.

## Lore

The Swan Castle, located near the Azure Lake, was the fief of Prince Alvin of the Moniyan Empire. The first liege of this land—Prince Alvin I—was a desperately romantic crown prince. To marry a woman of humble birth, he resigned and gave up the throne to his brother Sirius IV, leaving Lumina City and settling in the countryside now known as the Swan Castle.

House Alvin's romanticism was passed down through generations. Prince Alvin II assumed his royal title at a young age; his brother and the eldest son of Prince Alvin I focused on religious endeavors and later became the archbishop of the Church of Light. Princess Odette was the third-generation scion of the family: an elegant, lovable Swan Princess whose love story reflected House Alvin's romantic nature.

Being a branch of the royal family, Prince Alvin and his kin seldom involved themselves in domestic politics or had frequent contact with other nobles. Since childhood, Odette lived a peaceful life within the Swan Castle and had rarely ventured beyond its territory. Yet she was curious and longed for the outside world. In adolescence she began sneaking out in a mantle to visit the nearby market without her parents' permission. On one such outing she naively tried to pay for bread with a whole bag of gold coins and offered a beggar all her jewelry after hearing his tearful story—small rebellions that fed her appetite for adventure.

Those adventures eventually brought trouble. Harassed by thugs in a tavern, she was rescued by a drunken man who drew his sword and drove them away—but he was injured in the scuffle. Distressed to see her rescuer fall unconscious, Odette defied family rules and brought him back to Swan Castle.

In a tower room she dressed the man's wound and cast her family's healing spell with a scepter, aided by a young maid. She watched over him through the night until he finally opened his eyes. For days she hid the man in the castle so he could recover, bringing him food and listening to the romantic knight from Castle Gorge as he played the harp and sang of his adventures. The songs and their lyrics—unlike the royal music Odette had known—rekindled her curiosity and longing for the world beyond.

Odette's meetings with the knight became the highlight of her days. As she opened up, she recounted her own small rebellions and even learned a few sword techniques from him for self-defense. Over time, Lancelot became quieter and more withdrawn. He stopped playing his jaunty hero songs and instead often crooned about the love between a knight and a young maiden, praising a kind and beautiful princess.

One night, the maid whispered to Odette:

> "Who do you think Lancelot is playing these songs to, Your Grace?"

The maid's words awakened Odette. She realized someone was subtly courting her. Encouraged by the maid, Odette mustered the courage to face Lancelot's feelings, though she was unsure whether she loved him in return. As his injury healed, Odette knew his departure drew near. Her spirits sank further when a letter arrived informing her that Duke Baroque was proposing on behalf of his son—an unwelcome suitor. Recognizing that the "romantic knight of Castle Gorge" came from House Baroque's fief, she suspected the duke's son might be the knight.

Outwardly Odette feigned calm before her parents, but in private she giggled and danced with the maid. Unsure how to face Lancelot, she found herself avoiding him. He left a few days later with only a letter and no farewell. Dejected, Odette finally understood how deeply she cared.

One night she made up her mind: she wrote a letter to her parents and quietly left Swan Castle to follow the traces Lancelot had left behind and search for the love of her life.

- Special info / notes:
  - Magic basic attack; only benefits 50% of Magic Lifesteal.

## Trivia
- Odette's full name is Princess Odette Alvin.  
- The sword Odette carries is used as a focus for her spells; she doesn't use it by conventional means.  
- Since the founding of the empire by Gaius, the ruling Monian dynasty has remained the same. Silvanna, Odette, and Dyrroth share a common ancestor (Gaius).  
- There is a connection to Vexana, who was also a princess of the Monian Empire.  
- It is hinted in one of her quotes that the sword is a gift from Lancelot.  
- Lancelot is referenced in two of her quotes:
  > "Don't ask me why a mage is carrying a sword!"  
  > "This sword is a gift."
- Odette is inspired by the character of the same name in the ballet Swan Lake.  
- Her "Black Swan" skin references the Black Swan character from the same ballet/opera.  
- The music from her Ultimate, Swan Song, is from Tchaikovsky's Swan Lake (Ballet Suite, Op. 20).  
- She was initially purchasable with in-game currency; current acquisition methods include quests for new players, purchase in the Fragment Shop using Hero Fragments, or the Lucky Shop using Lucky Gems.  
- Odette is the only hero that cannot be bought using certain direct purchase currencies (specifics vary by region/version).  
- Repeated trivia note: The music from her Ultimate is from Tchaikovsky's Swan Lake (Ballet Suite).
