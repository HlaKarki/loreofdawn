Story

> "Forsaken Warden with sealed memories."

Created by the Ancient Ones, Edith was the Primal Warden that watched over the sealed Evil Gods on the Sanctum Island. Awakened and injured in an earthquake, she lost her memories and was saved by her foster father who made a living by selling scraps and continued to raise her for ten years.

When she once again helped her foster father enter the temple to search for scraps, the guardian of the ruins—Elemental Giant Uranus—was prepared to destroy the invaders. It was then that Edith remembered everything, but her humanity overpowered her divine nature, and she fought Uranus to protect her foster father. Just then, the Celestial Palace fell and the sealed Evil Gods scattered.

Edith parted with her foster father, drove her Mecha "Phylax" and set out on a journey to hunt down fleeing Evil Gods.

Lore

In the far distant past when the world was just created, Ancient Ones built the Celestial Palace above the Sanctum Island in the Sea of Hope. The island was part of a submarine volcano. Submerged rocks and atolls were strewn around the island and the nearby waters were often shrouded in dense sea fog, causing many shipwrecks.

Remote and undisturbed, the island was ideal for Ancient wonders. The Ancient Ones built temples and workshops with their advanced skills and technologies. When the Celestial Palace began functioning, the temple mechanisms on the island received energy waves from above, causing the island's systems to coordinate with the palace.

Before the Endless War, the Ancient Ones attempted to control the orders in the Land of Dawn. They sent Kaja and Uranus to arrest unruly Evil Gods and imprison them in the Temple of Thunder on Sanctum Island, where a priestess loyal to the Ancient Ones would drive her Mecha Guard "Phylax" and keep watch on them.

The Mecha was fueled by Primal Lightning, a unique magic that could control lightning bolts with Ancient power and could only be performed by those created by the Ancient Ones—among them Kaja and Uranus in the Celestial Palace and a priestess on Sanctum Island.

The priestess was named Edith. She accepted eternal youth and divine power granted by her masters and punished prisoners with ruthless Primal Lightning. Hearing her name alone could make even powerful Evil Gods tremble.

After the Endless War broke out, the Ancient Ones vanished after using the Twilight Orb to seal darkness and light. Edith, the immortal Primal Warden, fell into a deep slumber as her masters disappeared.

Times changed and some shipwreck survivors appeared on Sanctum Island. These impoverished people struggled to survive, often searching for scarce resources. After a devastating earthquake, the ruins of a temple were revealed—buried deep in a valley and scorched by lightning. While foraging for metal scraps, an old rag-man discovered a blond girl lying in the rubble. She was at risk of being struck by lightning.

The old man risked his life and carried the girl back to the seaside fishing village. Having been injured in the head, the girl could only remember her name—Edith. The childless old man took her in, and together they lived in the small village.

Edith's foster father doted on her and cared for her intensely. Edith could stroll the village when not helping with chores, but the old man had one rule: she couldn't go near the valley—he feared losing her there.

When Edith was bored she would often gaze up at the Celestial Palace above—the villagers said it was the temple of the gods, and sometimes she could see strange birdlike people gliding across the sky with lightning. Every time she saw them, unease filled her; the forgotten past seemed connected to lightning. She would run home and bury herself in her father's arms, calming down as he patted her hand.

Ten years passed and the old man grew frailer. The other kids aged, but Edith remained as young as when she was first found. Eventually the foster father was too old for physical labor and broke his own rule—he brought Edith with him to collect metal scraps in the valley ruins.

Having searched outside in vain, the old man stepped inside the temple with Edith. As they walked deeper, Edith felt memories surfacing. Suddenly lightning struck and giant flying blades shot at her father. Edith dashed forward and instinctively stopped the blades with her power; the blades plunged into the ground and split rocks with lightning sparks.

Amid the fire and dust an Elemental Giant in gold armor appeared before Edith.

> "You left your post, Edith!"

This was Uranus, the Guardian of the Celestial Palace. At that moment countless memories flooded Edith's mind: the Ancient Ones, priests and priestesses, the temple, Evil Gods… She screamed to reject the memories, but her mind was already engrossed in the past—awoken by the earthquake, raised by the old man when she barely knew anything.

> "Die, invader!"

Uranus waved his giant arm at the old man and fired flying blades. Quick as a flash, Phylax came as summoned and reached out its strong metal arm to catch the blades.

> "Let my father go."

Edith spoke from Phylax.

> "Traitor!"

Uranus shouted at the Mecha.

> "Or let's fight to the death."

Edith calmly replied.

Among the temple ruins in the valley, Edith and Uranus were locked in a fierce battle—roars of thunder echoed, lightning strikes crashed, and stone pillars and statues shattered. While the two were in deadlock, a birdlike Guardian of the Celestial Palace—Kaja—closed his wings and descended upon the ruins.

> "Stop the foolish fight! The Celestial Palace is in peril!"

They looked up at the sky: Edith couldn't sense the energy of the holy city anymore. Pieces began plummeting from the sky, and in a blink the great Celestial Palace started to collapse.

Since the palace fell, the temple on Sanctum Island could no longer receive energy from above and sealed Evil Gods began to flee.

> "Kaja, we shall stay and fix the seals! Edith, arrest the Evil Gods!"

Uranus commanded.

> "I understand my duty. Let me send my father back first!"

Edith answered.

Kaja and Uranus stayed behind in the valley to watch over the remaining Evil Gods, while Edith drove Phylax and took her foster father back to the village. As Edith stared at her senile, shaky father she couldn't hold back tears. After ten years with a family, she was no longer the cold and heartless Primal Warden.

"I'm his only child, his hope for the future. How will he go on living if I leave now?"

But the land faced new crises due to the fall of the Celestial Palace. Edith had to fulfill her duty and hunt down the escaped Evil Gods.

> "Farewell, dear father!"
> "Please trust me, I will one day return to you…"
> "I promise!"

Edith drove Phylax and flew toward the Land of Dawn. Later, many across the Land of Dawn claimed they had seen a giant Mecha Guard. Whenever an Evil God wreaked havoc, Edith and Phylax appeared instantly, punishing dark forces without mercy. Between battles the girl would detach from Phylax and gaze toward the far northeast; on the remote island an old man would also look longingly in his daughter's direction.

Trivia
- Edith is a feminine given name derived from Old English ēad (wealth/prosperity) + gȳð (strife/war); her name would technically mean "prosperous in war."
- Edith is the first hero to have the Tank/Marksman role.
- Edith was the 9th hero given to players for free on release.
- She is currently the only hero who does not have Attack speed growth.
- Aside from Brody, Edith is the only hero who doesn't wield any weapon.
- In Ancient Greek, phylax (φύλαξ) means “watcher, guard, sentinel, guardian, keeper, or protector.”
- Phylax was first introduced in advanced server; leaks and early-access servers revealed Edith alongside it.
- According to Phylax's old hero feature ("An ancient machine guard that executes any trespassers on the Sanctuary Island"), it was described as an executioner to trespassers. Phylax/Edith were supposed to come from Sanctuary Island (later named Sanctum Island).
- Phylax was described originally as an Ancient Machine rather than an Ancient Guard.
- Edith was originally planned for release on December 21, 2021 (Patch Notes 1.6.42) but was postponed and released on December 24, 2021.
