Story
As the eldest son of Duke Baroque, Lancelot was born rebellious. Dead set on resisting the political marriage arranged for him by his father, he traveled the country and led a licentious life instead. After being wounded at a drunken brawl in a tavern, Lancelot was rescued by Odette and finally came to realize what his heart truly yearned for. The prodigal son made a repentant return and thus began his adventure across the Land of Dawn.

Lore
In the southwest of the Moniyan Empire lies Castle Gorge, which had fallen into decline due to the depletion of its famed gold mines.

As the oldest noble in the Empire, House Baroque ruled over Castle Gorge. The eldest son of Duke Baroque — Lancelot — was a handsome young man with unmatched sword skills. He'd received an all-round good education but also inherited his father's snobbery and haughtiness.

House Baroque had been in decline for years, and the duke was relying on loans to keep up a façade. To revive his family, the duke came up with a plan — to choose a daughter-in-law with a handsome dowry and make a fortune out of Lancelot's marriage. Or so he thought…

Lancelot had been fascinated by charming knights and their heroic tales since childhood and believed that he was the most brilliant and charismatic knight of the modern time — so to him, it would besmirch his honor to make money off his own marriage. After a ridiculous duel with his father and a serious falling-out, Lancelot left home.

Even without his father's financial support, Lancelot made a living from his swordsmanship by attending contests across the country. He never lost a battle in the arena and would utter his family motto while signing his initials onto his opponent's body with his sword:
> "Thorns remain should petals fall"

Free of his father's control, Lancelot began fooling around with women, and stories of his romantic affairs spread throughout the empire. People jokingly called him the Blade of Roses. But Duke Baroque did not find it amusing and reportedly crossed Lancelot's name from their family records in anger.

While playing and traveling to the Azure Lake, Lancelot drank his fill in a tavern, then held a harp, played and sang songs in praise of himself. He noticed a few drunkards surrounding a young girl in a velvet mantle and drew his sword to help the harassed maiden — but because he was intoxicated, he was wounded in the attempt.

After driving away the drunken hooligans, Lancelot stumbled out of the tavern, knowing his injury might be fatal. Covered in blood, he dragged himself toward a tree and fell. Before losing consciousness he saw a pair of glowing swan wings — which later turned out to be the swan crown worn by the girl who saved him.

Lancelot woke to find himself bandaged and lying on a bed, an elegant young maiden smiling wearily at him. The girl used magic to help him heal; after a few days he was no longer at risk. He learned he had been in the Swan Castle of House Alvin, and the girl who saved him was Princess Odette.

It was forbidden to let outsiders into Swan Castle, so Odette had secretly hidden Lancelot in a room atop the castle tower. As he recovered, Odette delivered food to him each day with a young maid. At first Lancelot assumed Odette was enamored by his charisma; later he discovered she was the girl he helped in the tavern.

In the first days together, Lancelot continued telling half-truths about his swordsmanship and adventures, concealing his Baroque identity and calling himself "Lancelot, the romantic knight of Castle Gorge." Odette believed every poem he sang: about Castle Gorge, the Celestial Palace, bandits he'd defeated, or dark deities he'd only heard of. The curious princess, caged in the castle since childhood, was fascinated by the outside world.

Lancelot began to feel guilty about lying. When he could no longer boast before Odette, he realized he'd fallen madly in love with her. He regretted his promiscuous past but found it hard to reveal his feelings. When Odette visited, he would play the harp and sing passionate love songs. Odette at first didn't understand, then was unsure how to respond, and finally remained quiet and blushing.

In the days before Lancelot fully recovered, Odette did not appear once. Unable to see her, Lancelot's hands trembled while playing the harp. He asked the maid about Odette's absence. The maid replied with a cheerful smile:
> "Someone proposed to our princess. She'd been busy with it…"

Struck by the news, Lancelot broke a harp string in shock. He then asked the maid to send a letter to Odette.

The letter read:
> Dear Princess, please forgive me for departing without a farewell.  
> I've decided to travel to every mythical land as in the songs I've sung.  
> I promise that the day I return to the Swan Castle, I will stand before you as a true knight.  
> Hopefully, with the Azure Lake and bright sunlight as my witness, I will finally be able to tell you what I've been muttering to myself…

As Odette put down the letter and rushed to look out a window, Lancelot quietly left for a great journey across the land.

As brutal wars were waged throughout the Land of Dawn, the Blade of Roses was about to compose a song of praise that truly belonged to him.
