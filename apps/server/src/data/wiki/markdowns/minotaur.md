## Story
In olden times, there was once a state named Minoa in Agelta Drylands.  
The prince in the state, Minotaur fatally injured his father in a fit of rage, after which he exiled himself to the Minoan Labyrinth in shame and regret.  
When he stepped out of the Labyrinth again, he found his state had long perished, his kin long gone.  
With his teeth set, Monitor embark on the path to restoring his homeland to its former glory.

## Lore
In olden times, the Minoans founded a mysterious and prosperous state named Minoa in Agelta Drylands.

The once-bustling Emerald Road connected Minoa with other western cities, creating a network of trade routes, while Minoa stood tall among all others with its advanced civilization and martial traditions.

Prince Minotaur of Minoa was the most esteemed warrior back then. Passionate about fighting and often fueled by his rage, Minotaur would demonstrate terrifying bursts of strength in battle. Few were those who could even hope to last more than a few rounds against him.

The people grew to worship Minotaur, yet his father was deeply concerned about him. While reaping great success on the battlefield, Minotaur's savagery and impulsive behavior was unbecoming of the successor of the state.

To forge Minotaur into a suitable heir to the throne, the old king arranged for a special trial—one in which the prince must defeat a mysterious figure from foreign lands to qualify for the throne of Minoa.

Minotaur accepted this unarmed duel without hesitation. He stepped onto the arena with his head held high and attempted to crush his opponent with brute force as he'd always done. However, his opponent this time was much tougher, and Minotaur had soon exhausted himself, losing his advantage. In the end, the mysterious figure defeated the "invincible" Minotaur with measured counterattacks.

The crowd cheered for the strong and graceful victor, yet Minotaur was overwhelmed by rage and shame. And when the mysterious figure stretched out his hand to help Minotaur get back to his feet, Minotaur grabbed the unsuspecting hand and pinned the grabbed the unsuspecting hand and pinned the mysterious figure to the ground, before throwing a series of vicious punches at him.

As his fists were covered in blood and the mysterious figure stopped struggling, Minotaur tore off the latter's mask amidst the boos of the crowd—and that was when his whole body stiffened. The severely injured figure on the ground was actually his father!

> "You must learn to shoulder the responsibility as a king and continue the glory of Minoa..."

Consumed by shame and regret, Minotaur headed to the Minoan Labyrinth—the burial ground of the Minoan ancestors—and began his training and repentance. Over time, Minotaur gradually gained control over his rage and became able to tap into his raging powers at will.

As Minotaur thrust himself into this arduous training, an unexpected catastrophe struck. The entire Minoa was flattened by the explosion of the Twilight Orb, and only Minotaur was left unharmed under the protection of the Labyrinth. Minotaur rushed toward the state amidst the tremors, wanting to find out what had happened, when a broken piece from the Twilight Orb plummeted into the Labyrinth and sealed it whole. Minotaur, who was half a step out of the Labyrinth, was sealed altogether and put into a deep slumber.

The catastrophe erased all traces of the Minoan civilization, leaving only the Minoan Labyrinth half-hidden in the sand. After countless days and nights, the broken piece from the Twilight Orb turned into Lunox and left.

The seal was then lifted. Time in the Labyrinth began to flow again, while Minotaur slowly opened his eyes....

Aldous, the guardian of the Labyrinth, told Minotaur his state had long turned into dust, and he and his memories were all that remained. Minotaur, however, would believe none of those.

With his father's final entrustment still fresh in his mind, the prince vowed to himself that he must find his lost kin and rebuild the Minoan civilization.

In the boundless sands, a set of hoof-prints extended into the horizon...

## Scourge of the Gods (retcon notes)
There are notable deviations between the "Scourge of the Gods" comic (released 2018) and Minotaur's story (updated 2021):

- In the 2018 comic, Minotaur rushed outside the briefly protected labyrinth and witnessed the aftermath of the divine punishment of Minoa and fled cowardly from the catastrophe. In the 2021 story, Minotaur cannot go outside the labyrinth as "a broken piece from the Twilight Orb plummeted into the Labyrinth and sealed it whole," trapping him for, presumably, a long time.
- The comic shows Minotaur meeting Aldous after coming back to the labyrinth. The 2021 story suggests instead that Minotaur met Aldous after the seal within the labyrinth has been lifted. This is supported by the fact that Minotaur was the only one trapped inside the Labyrinth, and Aldous is not even mentioned in the comic.

Main appearance: Scourge of the Gods

## Trivia
- His name comes from the Minotaur, a legendary creature in Greek mythology with the body of a man and the head of a bull.
- Minotaur's real name is Asterion, inspired and taken from Astraeus. His name Minotaur was given when he became the strongest Minotaur of Minoan.
- The name Astraeus comes from the Greek word "Ἀστερίων" meaning "Starry One", suggesting an association with the bull constellation Taurus.
- Minotaur is the only tank to heal allies.
- His skin Orbiter was originally called Taurus, but was later changed because it would collide with his Zodiac skin, Taurus. (Orbiter, Taurus)
- Early concept art showed a more primitive stone hammer, but later versions made it look more refined and enchanted, possibly hinting at a magical origin.
- He is the first hero with a "Rage Bar" mechanic in MLBB. Unlike most heroes who have a mana or energy system, Minotaur's abilities become stronger when his Rage Bar is full, making him a unique tank. This mechanic has since been revised so that his skills are enhanced when he uses his previously enhanced Ultimate.
